Car Rental Terms:

  1. Age Limit:

    Car rental companies have age restrictions for renters, usually ranging from 21 to 25 years of age, and sometimes up to 30 years old, depending on the company. Renters under the age limit may be required to pay a surcharge.

  2. Driver's License:

    Renters must hold a valid driver's license. Some car rental companies may also require an international driving permit (IDP) for foreign renters.

  3. Payment:

    Car rental companies may require a deposit or a credit card to secure the rental. The rental rate may also vary depending on the duration of the rental and the type of vehicle rented.

  4. Insurance:

    Car rental companies usually offer insurance coverage for the rental vehicle. This coverage may include collision damage waiver, theft protection, and liability insurance. The renter can also purchase additional coverage if desired.

  5. Additional Drivers:

    Additional drivers may be added to the rental agreement for an additional fee. These drivers must also meet the age and driver's license requirements.

  6. Rental Period:

    The rental period may be charged on a daily or weekly basis. Late returns may result in additional charges.

  7. Damage and Fees:

    Renters are responsible for any damage to the rental vehicle during the rental period. Additional fees may be charged for excessive mileage, additional drivers, or other violations of the rental agreement.

Bike Rental Terms

  1. Age limit:

    Most bike rental companies have a minimum age limit of 18 years. This is because it is illegal for minors to enter into a contract.

  2. Identification:

    Customers are required to provide valid identification, such as a driver's license or passport, when renting a bike.

  3. Rental period:

    The rental period is the amount of time the customer will have access to the bike. This is usually calculated in hours, days or weeks.

  4. Payment:

    The customer must pay the rental fee upfront, and provide a valid credit card as security.

  5. Security deposit:

    Some rental companies may require a security deposit to cover any damage or loss of the bike.

  6. Insurance:

    It is recommended that customers purchase insurance to protect themselves against any damage or loss of the bike.

  7. Maintenance:

    The customer is responsible for maintaining the bike during the rental period. This includes keeping it clean and in good condition, and reporting any damage or malfunctions to the rental company.

  8. Liability:

    The customer is responsible for any damage or injury caused by their use of the bike. It is recommended that customers purchase liability insurance to protect themselves against any legal claims.
